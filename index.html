Biography

Early Life

  1. Born 17 August 1878 in Rutland Square, Dublin
  2. Attended Christian Brothers' O'Connell School (North Richmond Street, Dublin)1890-92. He then went to Mungret College, a boarding school near Limerick. The following year he transferred to Stonyhurst College in Lancashire, England. He returned to Ireland in 1896 and boarded at Clongowes Wood College while studying for examinations with the Royal University of Ireland.
  3. He was a talented athlete; in England he had briefly played for the Preston North End FC Reserve, and while at Clongowes he played for the Bohemian FC. He also played on Clongowes's soccer and cricket elevens.
  4. 1898 he switched to the medical school at Trinity College, having failed eight of his ten examinations at the Royal
  5. While at university He became interested in Irish nationalism after meeting Arthur Griffith in 1899, and contributed propaganda pieces to The United Irishman over subsequent years
